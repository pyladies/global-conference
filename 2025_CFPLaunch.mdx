## PyLadiesCon 2025: Call for Proposals is Now Open!

We are excited to announce that the Call for Proposals (CFP) for PyLadiesCon 2025 is officially open! 
We can’t wait to build this year’s conference with you.

PyLadiesCon 2025 is a global online conference, designed to promote diversity, learning and empowerment within the Python software community. To catch a 
glimpse of our past events, check out [PyLadiesCon 2023](https://2023.conference.pyladies.com/) and [PyLadiesCon 2024](https://2024.conference.pyladies.com/).
This year is going to be bigger and we are inviting you to be a part of it by submitting a talk proposal.

- 📅 Conference Date: December 5 – 7, 2025
- 🌍 Location: Online, accessible to PyLadies around the globe
- 🗓️ CFP Opens: July 11, 2025
- 🗓️ CFP Closes: August 15, 2025
- 🔗 Submit your proposal here: CFP Portal

### Why Speak at PyLadiesCon?
PyLadiesCon is a conference organized by PyLadies, for PyLadies and friends, with a mission to amplify the voices of women and gender minorities in Python globally.
Speaking at PyLadiesCon is not just about sharing your knowledge; it is about inspiring others, building your confidence, and helping our community grow together. Your perspective matters whether you’re a first-time speaker or experienced.

### What We’re Looking For
We welcome proposals on any topic of interest to the PyLadies community (introductory to advanced), including but not limited to:
- Python programming
- Data science and machine learning
- Web development
- Automation and tooling
- Open source and community building
- Career journeys and mentorship
- Accessibility and ethics in tech
- Non-code contributions in Python
- Your personal Python journey


We especially encourage first-time speakers and PyLadies from underrepresented regions to submit!
If you need inspiration, check out last year’s talks, or sign up for our “Craft a conference proposal” workshop [here](https://lu.ma/nkkeq1lz).

### How to Submit
Visit our [CFP Portal](https://pretalx.com/pyladiescon-2025/cfp) to submit your proposal. 
You can propose Talks (20-30 minutes), Workshops (40-60 minutes), Panels (20-30 minutes)
